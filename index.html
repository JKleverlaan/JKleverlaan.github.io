VR motion learning

VR motion learning are an austrain startup company who have been working on a tennis vr game, which will allow people to play and train from home. The software measures all your movements and thinks of the best way to improve your game. they think that with the help of this technology both casual tennis players and proffesionals will be able to improve way faster and it will ofcourse be availible from home so even if you don't have a tenniscourt close to your home, you will still be able to play.

The Pitchlogic baseball

The pitchlogic ball is a baseball made to improve your throws. The pitchlogic ball has a sensor in it, that sends data about your throw to a app that calculates how you can improve your game the best. it uses wireless charging to stay charged and to connect the ball to your phone you just have to hold the ball to the back of your phone. This app is also used by scouts and coaches to find the best players. Pitchlogic works with a pay per month system so you can always stop.

The Hawk-eye

The hawk-eye is a computer and camera set that traces balls. It is used as a support tool for referees in everything from, soccer to cricket. They also help sports commentators talk about the most important parts of a game. One of it's main uses is speeding up decision making by referees through instant replay and slowmotion to look at what exactly happends at all times. The also use the Multi-Angle Replay System or MARS for short to send extra footage to broadcasters, so the viewers from home have a greater viewing experience aswell.
